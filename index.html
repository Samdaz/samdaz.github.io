Hi! I’m Samuel D. Escribano, a Postdoc fellow working in the Condensed Matter Physics Department at The Weizmann Institute of Science, in Yuval Oreg’s group.

I’m a theoretical/computational physicist studying exotic states in low-dimensional superconductor-semiconductor heterostructures. I’m interested on their possible topological properties and potential applications in quantum computing. I’m an expert on performing numerical simulations of realistic devices, usually with a close collaboration with experimental groups. I’m always open to talk and collaborate!

Research interests: low-dimensional materials, superconductivity, topological materials, quantum computing.
